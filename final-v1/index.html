USEF: Governing Body of U.S. Equestrian Sports

The U.S. Equestrian Federation (USEF) is the national governing body for equestrian sports in the United States, recognized by both the United States Olympic & Paralympic Committee (USOPC) and the International Federation for Equestrian Sports (FEI). Originally founded as the American Horse Shows Association in 1917, USEF has evolved to represent a wide array of equestrian disciplines, including dressage, show jumping, eventing, and many others. USEF’s primary mission is to set standards for fair competition and promote excellence across all levels of equestrian sports, from local shows to the Olympic Games. By regulating over 2,500 events annually and establishing rules that ensure the welfare of horses and the safety of riders, USEF has become a cornerstone of the American equestrian community. Today, the organization is home to more than 100,000 members, who rely on USEF’s governance to maintain the integrity of the sport and support its growth nationwide.

Safety First: USEF’s Commitment to Safe Sport and Horse Welfare

In recent years, the U.S. Equestrian Federation (USEF) has led the charge in promoting safe and ethical practices within equestrian sports. A key part of this mission is the Safe Sport Policy, which was introduced in 2018 to ensure the safety of all participants. This policy mandates that all USEF members complete training designed to prevent abuse and harassment, with a strong focus on protecting minors in the sport. In addition to human safety, USEF prioritizes the welfare of horses. The federation enforces strict anti-doping rules and has implemented guidelines for the humane treatment of competition horses. From regulating competition practices to monitoring horse health and well-being, USEF is deeply committed to creating a safe and supportive environment for all equestrian athletes. Through these efforts, USEF seeks to uphold the highest ethical standards and foster a culture of respect within the equestrian community.

Supporting Equestrian Athletes: USEF’s High-Performance Programs

The U.S. Equestrian Federation (USEF) plays a crucial role in developing and supporting top-tier equestrian talent across the United States. Through its High-Performance Programs, USEF provides resources and training opportunities for athletes aiming to compete at the highest levels of international competition, including the Olympics, Paralympics, and World Equestrian Games. These programs offer access to elite coaching, sports science, and funding opportunities, helping riders and their horses reach their full potential. USEF’s High-Performance Programs have been instrumental in securing U.S. medals and fostering the growth of competitive equestrian disciplines. Whether training riders for their first national championship or preparing them for world stages, USEF is dedicated to helping American equestrian athletes succeed.

The USEF also supports athletes by providing pathways for both amateur and elite riders, offering developmental programs, scholarships, and training opportunities to help riders grow and succeed. Through initiatives like the USEF High Performance Program, aspiring Olympians and international competitors gain access to coaching, clinics, and support that prepare them for the world stage. Additionally, USEF fosters community among members. By offering resources, hosting events, and creating a network of shared knowledge and support, USEF helps to create a positive environment for equestrian enthusiasts across the nation. Membership includes benefits such as competition licenses, educational resources, and access to exclusive content, which enhances the value and accessibility of equestrian sports. Through its commitment to integrity, safety, and athlete development, USEF not only advances competitive equestrian sports but also ensures that they continue to grow in a way that is sustainable, ethical, and inclusive. By balancing tradition with innovation, the U.S. Equestrian Federation remains a vital part of the American equestrian landscape.

USEF: National Leadership

USEF serves as the national governing body for equestrian sports in the United States. Its primary focus is to regulate and promote equestrian disciplines within the country, ensuring fairness, safety, and excellence in competition. USEF sets national standards, provides education and training, and supports athletes across various levels, from grassroots programs to elite competitions. As a representative body, USEF also facilitates the selection of riders and teams for international events.

F.E.I: Global Governance

The FEI, based in Lausanne, Switzerland, is the international governing body for equestrian sports worldwide. Established in 1921, the FEI oversees disciplines such as dressage, show jumping, eventing, and more at the global level. It establishes rules and standards for international competitions, ensuring uniformity and fairness. The FEI also organizes major events like the FEI World Equestrian Games and oversees equestrian events at the Olympics.
